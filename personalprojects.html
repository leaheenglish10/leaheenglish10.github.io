Binary Mass Transfer

  • Link to Poster
  • Link to AAS Poster

Blue Straggler Stars are stars that appear more luminous and bluer, and therefore younger than expected. One theory for their formation is through binary systems that have undergone mass transfer, as many of them are observed to be in a binary themselves. My models explore how two observed binary systems consisting of Blue Straggler Stars, WOCS 5379 and WOCS 14020, may evolve with their observed binary companions undergoing various types of mass transfer. These systems are interesting as they may be progenitor systems to current objects whose origins we are currently unsure of.

Stellar Streams

  • Link to AAS Poster
  • Link to Poster

Stellar Streams are a group of stars orbiting a galaxy's outer edges and are likely formed via merging globular clusters or dwarf galaxies that have been tidally stripped. My work analyzes simulations of these streams, to see what these streams may tell us about a galaxy's mass distribution and formation history. In addition, I compare different particle spray methods of modelling stellar streams to help compare to the accuracy of a new stellar stream production code.
